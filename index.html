About

A specialist, not a generalist

Laura Krentel

Literacy Specialist  ·  Associate Certified, Orton Gillingham Academy  ·  Reading Specialist

I work with students who struggle with literacy — not because they aren't trying, but because their brains process language differently. For twenty years, I have worked with families to understand why a child is struggling and build a precise, individualized plan to help them move forward.

Literacy is not just reading. It is the full, interconnected web of skills that allows a child to make sense of language — and to use it with confidence and joy. My approach is holistic: I work across reading, writing, vocabulary, grammar, and language comprehension, because these skills are inseparable. Strong readers become strong writers. Students who love words love to learn.

My instruction is grounded in Orton-Gillingham and the science of reading — structured, multisensory, and always diagnostic. Every student begins with a comprehensive literacy assessment, because good instruction starts with knowing exactly where a child is.

I currently serve as a Literacy Specialist at an independent school in NW DC, and work with private students in NW DC, Bethesda, and surrounding areas. Zoom sessions are available in select circumstances.

Services

What I offer

Every student is different. My services are designed to meet a child where they are — whether that means targeted OG instruction, a comprehensive literacy assessment, or broad support across reading, writing, and language.

Orton-Gillingham Instruction

Structured, multisensory, individualized literacy instruction for students with dyslexia or language-based reading difficulties. Lessons are diagnostic and prescriptive — built around each student's specific profile and adjusted as they progress.

Literacy Assessment & Report

A comprehensive evaluation of phonics, phonological awareness, fluency, comprehension, and spelling — scored against grade-level benchmarks and compiled into a detailed written report with individualized instructional recommendations.

Initial assessment fee: $250

Broad Literacy Support

Targeted support across the full scope of literacy — reading fluency and comprehension, writing, grammar, vocabulary, and morphology. Particularly well-suited for upper elementary and middle school students developing academic language skills.

Families I have worked with come from St. Albans, Little Flower, Holton Arms, Montgomery County Public Schools, and independent schools throughout NW DC and Bethesda. Please reach out using the form below — I am happy to discuss whether my practice is a good fit.

How it works

From first contact to lasting progress

Working with a literacy specialist should feel clear, not overwhelming. Here is what the process looks like from start to finish.

1

Initial conversation

We start with a brief call or email exchange so I can understand your child's history, your concerns, and what you're hoping for. This helps me determine whether my practice is the right fit and what kind of support makes sense.

2

Comprehensive literacy assessment

Before any instruction begins, I conduct a thorough evaluation — phonics knowledge, phonological awareness, reading fluency, comprehension, and spelling. This gives us an accurate baseline and tells us exactly where to focus. The assessment fee is $250 and includes a full written report.

3

Written report & individualized plan

You receive a detailed written report with scored results, a clear explanation of what they mean, and a specific instructional plan. This document is yours — it can be shared with your child's school, their teachers, or other specialists.

4

Ongoing sessions

Sessions are structured, multisensory, and always built around the student in front of me. For students working on foundational skills, twice-weekly sessions are recommended to support retention. Frequency is always discussed and adjusted based on your child's needs and your family's schedule.

5

Progress monitoring & updated reports

I reassess regularly and share updated data reports so you can see exactly how your child is progressing. These aren't just numbers — they tell a story about what has changed, what it means, and what comes next.
